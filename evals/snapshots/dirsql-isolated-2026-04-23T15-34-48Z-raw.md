Summary: dirsql's current release machinery is ~1,700 YAML LOC + 200 Python LOC across two parallel flows (patch-release/publish + cargo-dist/publish-npm). piot would cleanly replace the three Python decision scripts, most of `publish.yml`, the idempotency plumbing, and the OIDC wiring — the concepts/config pages name "the dirsql shape" as the canonical handoff. **The blocker is a documented piot non-feature:** piot cannot publish a single top-level npm package whose `optionalDependencies` mix both CLI (`bundled-cli`) and napi (`napi`) platform families — and that's exactly what `packages/ts/package.json` ships today (`"bin": {...}` + `"napi": {...}` under one `dirsql` name, with `publish-npm.yml:165-171` synthesizing both families under it). You either split `dirsql` into `dirsql` + `dirsql-cli` on npm (breaking change), extend piot upstream to support the combined shape (the fix that generalizes to future consumers), or limit piot adoption to crates+PyPI and keep the npm flow as-is (weakest — preserves most of the work you wanted to delete).
